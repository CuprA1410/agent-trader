# Market Regimes Playbook

*Do not re-derive regime language from scratch every cycle. Use these definitions and trade rules.*

---

## 1. Trending Up

### Definition

- BTC 4H is making higher highs and higher lows
- Pullbacks hold prior support or reclaim it quickly
- Breakouts expand on volume instead of failing immediately

### What To Trade

- Long pullbacks into support, EMA, or reclaim zones
- Long retests after breakout
- Strong alts that hold up during BTC pauses

### What To Avoid

- Counter-trend shorts without extreme confluence
- Buying extended candles far from support
- Chasing breakout highs without a retest

### Confirmation Clues

- 1H pullback volume declines
- Bounce candles close strong
- BTC holds above reclaimed levels

### Invalidation

- 4H higher low breaks
- Multiple 1H closes back below the reclaimed level
- Breakout bar is immediately reversed by a larger opposite-volume bar

---

## 2. Trending Down

### Definition

- BTC 4H is making lower highs and lower lows
- Rally attempts fail into prior support turned resistance
- Selloffs expand on volume and bounces are weak

### What To Trade

- Shorts on relief rallies into supply
- Shorts on failed reclaim of key BTC level
- Alt shorts when BTC confirms weakness and the alt shows relative weakness

### What To Avoid

- Trying to bottom-pick just because RSI is low
- Closing strong macro shorts on weak, low-volume bounces
- Longs in the middle of breakdown continuation

### Confirmation Clues

- High-volume breakdown bar
- Low-volume dead-cat bounce
- Confirmed close below trigger level
- ETH or SOL underperform BTC during the bounce

### Invalidation

- BTC reclaims the breakdown level on strong volume
- A macro invalidation rule triggers on confirmed close
- Relative weakness disappears and alts start outperforming into the bounce

---

## 3. Range / Chop

### Definition

- BTC swings between clear boundaries without follow-through
- Breaks above or below range fail quickly
- Volume contracts overall and directional conviction is low

### What To Trade

- Only the range extremes with tight invalidation
- Retests of failed breakouts if volume confirms rejection
- Otherwise: stay in cash

### What To Avoid

- Trading the middle of the range
- Calling every wick a trend reversal
- Taking alt trades when BTC has no directional commitment

### Confirmation Clues

- Repeated rejection of the same boundaries
- Several bars closing back inside the same range
- No clean expansion after breakout attempts

### Invalidation

- High-volume close outside the range followed by continuation
- Successful retest of broken boundary

---

## 4. Post-Flush Bounce

### Definition

- BTC prints a decisive flush down bar or liquidation-style spike
- Price bounces immediately after the flush low
- The bounce may be real recovery or just reflexive mean reversion

### What To Trade

- Short supply-zone failures after the bounce if BTC cannot reclaim broken levels
- Longs only after BTC reclaims and holds key levels on volume

### What To Avoid

- Shorting the exact flush low
- Longing the first bounce candle just because it is green
- Treating a partial recovery as a full regime change

### Confirmation Clues For Short Bias

- BTC fails at the reclaim zone
- ETH or another alt prints large supply bars during the bounce
- Re-break below the key level happens on volume

### Confirmation Clues For Long Bias

- BTC reclaims and holds above the failed-breakdown level
- Follow-through closes confirm the recovery
- Alts stop lagging and begin holding pullbacks

### Invalidation

- Bounce sustains above reclaim zone with volume
- Supply rejection never appears

---

## 5. Regime-to-Action Matrix

| Regime | Primary Bias | Best Setup Type | Risk Posture |
|---|---|---|---|
| Trending Up | Long | Pullback / reclaim / retest | Normal |
| Trending Down | Short | Relief rally into supply | Normal to aggressive |
| Range / Chop | Selective or flat | Extremes only | Reduced |
| Post-Flush Bounce | Wait for confirmation | Reclaim or failed reclaim | Reduced until confirmed |

---

## 6. Language Rules

Use these terms consistently:

- **Failed breakdown wick:** a lower excursion that closes back inside or above the level
- **Shooting star / rejection bar:** upper wick rejection at supply with weak close
- **Breakdown continuation:** breakdown bar, weak bounce, then confirmed close below trigger
- **Supply-zone rejection:** bounce into resistance/supply, then bearish rejection candle forms there

If you are calling the same structure by different names in adjacent cycles, stop and map it back to one of the regimes above first.
