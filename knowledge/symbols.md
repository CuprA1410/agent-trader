Every coin has a personality. BTC moves differently from SOL, which moves differently from XRP.

- How volatile is it? (ATR relative to price)
- Does it respect S/R levels well, or does it overshoot?
- How correlated is it to BTC?

## Pre-Seeded Notes (General Knowledge)

### BTC (Bitcoin)
- Market leader. All other coins follow BTC's direction.
- Very liquid — S/R levels are respected more than smaller coins
- Lower volatility relative to price compared to altcoins
- Large moves often happen at Asian open (02:00-04:00 UTC) or US open (13:00-15:00 UTC)
- Major psychological levels: $50K, $60K, $70K, $80K, $100K etc. act as strong S/R

### ETH (Ethereum)
- Second most liquid after BTC
- Highly correlated to BTC (~80-90%) — if BTC dumps, ETH usually dumps harder
- ETH/BTC ratio: when this goes up, ETH outperforms BTC (altseason signal)
- Slightly more volatile than BTC percentage-wise

### SOL (Solana)
- More volatile than BTC and ETH
- Can make 5-10% moves in a day during active markets
- Less liquid — S/R levels can overshoot significantly
- Strong narrative-driven coin (ecosystem news can cause large moves)
- During altseason often outperforms ETH

### XRP (Ripple)
- Highly volatile and narrative-driven (lawsuit news, SEC developments)
- Can have massive moves (20-30%+) triggered by news
- Be cautious with wide SLs — volatility can be extreme
- Strong community — often pumps independently of BTC

### General Altcoin Notes
- Altcoins are more volatile than BTC
- During BTC downturns, alts often fall harder (higher beta)
- During BTC uptrends, alts often rally harder (higher beta upside too)
- Altcoin season: when BTC dominance falls, altcoins rally relative to BTC
